# Hypothesis Structuring Card

## Purpose

This card defines how an aging biology hypothesis is converted into a
population-scale human genetics question before statistical analysis.

## Inputs

- Candidate molecule, pathway, or cell/tissue axis.
- Proposed biological direction.
- Measurable molecular evidence layer, such as expression or protein abundance.
- Aging-related phenotype classes suitable for GWAS-based analysis.

## Decision Rules

1. Define the exposure as a genetically instrumentable molecular trait when
   possible.
2. Define the outcome as a population-scale aging phenotype with accessible
   summary statistics.
3. Separate tissue regulatory evidence from circulating molecular regulation.
4. Treat the resulting analysis as exploratory unless independent replication
   and strong colocalization evidence are available.

## CRELD2 Application

The adipose-liver secretory stress hypothesis was translated into the question
of whether genetic regulation of circulating CRELD2 is associated with
aging-related outcomes. Tissue eQTL evidence was used to contextualize
regulatory relevance, while pQTL, MR, and colocalization analyses were used for
population-scale genetic evaluation.
