# Hypothesis Structuring Card

## Purpose

This card defines how a biological aging claim is converted into competing,
single-cell-testable hypotheses before analysis.

## Inputs

- Candidate molecule, pathway, or cell/tissue axis.
- Proposed biological direction and the cell types it implicates.
- The single-cell observable that distinguishes the competing mechanisms, such
  as cell-type expression, age trajectory, or cell-cell interaction.
- Young and old age groups and any required sex stratification.

## Decision Rules

1. Frame the claim as competing hypotheses (A vs B) that the atlas can
   adjudicate, each with an explicit single-cell prediction.
2. Define the analysis scope: tissue, gene or genes, candidate cell types, young
   and old age groups, sex stratification, and analysis intent.
3. Choose the analysis intent (lineage screening, subcluster phenotyping,
   trajectory, enrichment, or cell-cell interaction) that the hypotheses require.
4. Treat the resulting analysis as supportive of cellular origin or context, not
   causal.

## Application

The CIRBP liver claim was structured as a cell-of-origin contrast: hepatocyte
intrinsic stress (A) versus Kupffer/myeloid immune activation (B) for the
age-dependent rise in liver Cirbp. The PGAT claim was structured as a niche
contrast: male-specific VEC niche deterioration and VEGF-axis loss driving ASPC
regenerative decline (A) versus a sex-nonspecific/conserved process (B), with
VEC and ASPC as candidate cell types and sex stratification by both sexes.
